Throughout History

1843

Ada Lovelace

Regarded by many as the first programmer, Ada Lovelace paved the way for all computer scientists. Taking a mathematical approach to programming, she introduced the concept of a computer program to the world.

1848

Seneca Falls Convention

Held in Seneca Falls, NY, this meeting launched the beginning of the women's suffrage movement in the United States. Women later won the right to vote in 1920.

1911

International Women's Day

In March of 1911, the first International Women's Day was celebrated.

1963

Valentina Tereshkova

On June 16, 1963, Valentina Tereshkova became the first woman to go to space.

1981

Sandra Day O' Connor

Appointed by President Ronald Reagan in 1981, Sandra Day O' Connor became the first woman to serve on the Supreme Court.

1996

UN Annual Theme

The UN announced their first annual theme "Celebrating the past, Planning for the Future" which was followed in 1997 with "Women at the Peace table", in 1998 with "Women and Human Rights", in 1999 with "World Free of Violence Against Women", and so on each year.

2012

Malala Yousafzai

Malala, a Pakistani advocate for girls' education, survived an assassination attempt by the Taliban and continued to champion education for girls, becoming the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate.

2017

#MeToo Movement

The Me Too Movement, which was an awareness movement around the issue of sexual harassment and sexual abuse of women in the workplace, gained prominence in 2017.

2018

Women's Day Marches

In 2018, women around the world participated in marches for political and social change and equality

2021

Kamala Harris

Kamala Harris becomes the first woman and person of color to be inaugurated as the Vice President of the United States. She also became the highest ranking woman in the entire country.
